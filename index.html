Is There a Correlation Between Crime Type and Victim Age and Gender in LA?

Introduction to Project

Crime is frequently influenced by demographic factors such as age and gender. This indicates the significance of understanding the relationship between crime type and victim age and gender in any city, to yield insights into strategies and initiatives to enforce the law and prevent crime. Notable examples of crime type affecting certain demographics include scamming disproportionately affecting older populations and violence affecting younger populations. Likewise, males and females may experience different crimes at differing rates. By analyzing crime data in Los Angeles, we can identify patterns that help policymakers and law enforcement create solutions and better allocate resources. Moreover, understanding these correlations sheds light on potential vulnerabilities of different demographics and deepens our knowledge of the social dynamics influencing crime.

Introduction to Data

This LA crime dataset is sourced from the U.S. Government’s Data Catalog and contains 9,999 records of reported crimes in Los Angeles from July 2, 2024 to August 9, 2024 (due to the 10,000 row limit, a wider range of time can’t be included). This dataset provides information on crime incidents, including but not limited to report numbers, dates, times, geographical location, crime type, descriptions, classifications, victim age, victim gender, victim ethnicity, and weapon involvement, across 29 columns of data using a variety of data types (integers, floats, and strings), making it a helpful resource for analyzing crime in Los Angeles to provide insights for policymakers and law enforcement.
